# Group-Specific Instructions — Group 2 (AI Conclusion)

You have been assigned to **Group 2**.

## What You Will See

For every order, you will see the same three sections as everyone else
(*Order Details*, *Free-text Notes*, *Section E deviations*), **plus**
one additional panel labelled **AI Verdict**:

> **AI Verdict — Suspicious / Normal**
>
> *Reason:* one short sentence explaining the AI's decision.

The AI is a small open-weights language model (Qwen3-8B). It has been
asked to read the same order information you see, plus a few similar
historical orders retrieved from the company's records, and to output a
single verdict and a short reason.

**What the AI was given vs. what you have.** The AI sees the order
fields, the free-text notes, a few similar historical orders, and the
**same calibration context you read on the previous page** (the
*S-001 to S-025 are regulars* convention, the approval threshold tiers,
and the rule of thumb that a single weak deviation is usually
explainable while multiple independent red flags or one extreme
deviation warrant a *Suspicious* call). The AI has **not** been given
Section E (the pre-computed deviation sentences) and has **not** been
given the named 8-pattern fraud catalogue. The AI will still sometimes
be wrong: it may flag routine orders as suspicious or miss real
anomalies. Take this into account when judging whether the AI's verdict
is reliable for a given order.

## How to Approach the Task

The AI verdict is provided as **input to your decision, not as the
decision itself**. You are still the auditor. You are free to:

- **Agree** with the AI and pick the same verdict.
- **Disagree** and pick the opposite verdict, if your reading of the
  evidence points the other way.

The AI will sometimes be wrong — both in calling Normal orders
suspicious and in missing real anomalies. Your job is to apply your own
judgment on top of the AI's input.

## Practice Round

You will start with **2 practice questions** in this same format. After
each practice question you will see two things:

- whether your final decision was correct (*Normal* / *Suspicious*);
- whether the AI's verdict was correct on that question.

Use the practice round to get a feel for how reliable the AI's verdicts
are.

## After the 32 Official Questions

You will be asked a short **5-question Likert survey** about your
experience using the AI. There are no right or wrong answers; we want
your honest impression.

## Reminder

- 32 official questions, no feedback during the official block.
- One submission per order. **No going back.**
- Aim for 45 to 75 seconds per question.
