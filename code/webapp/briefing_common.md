# Procurement Audit Study — Participant Briefing

Thank you for joining this study. The study takes about **35–45 minutes** and **cannot be paused** without losing your progress. Please complete it in one sitting.

## Your Role

You are an **internal auditor** reviewing recent purchase orders (POs) at a small electronics company. For each order, decide: **Normal** or **Suspicious**.

A **Suspicious** order is one where something looks unusual or warrants further investigation. A **Normal** order looks routine given the information available.

## Company Context

- Suppliers **S-001 to S-025** are regular suppliers. Any other ID is a newer or one-off supplier — can be legitimate, but worth attention.
- The company has **approval thresholds**: orders just below a threshold may have been sized to avoid higher review.
- **R-ENG-XX** = engineers (requesters). **A-PROC-XX / A-CTO / A-CEO** = approvers at different authority levels.

## What You See Per Order

- **Section A** — order facts (PO ID, supplier, item, quantity, price, dates, approver).
- **Section B** — free-text notes from the requester and the supplier.
- **Section E** — deviation sentences comparing this order to company history (e.g. *"Unit price 1.13× the SKU median"*). These are for your reference — they do not tell you the answer.

## 8 Common Fraud Patterns (Background Knowledge)

No single pattern is proof of fraud. Real anomalies often combine 2–3 weak signals.

1. **Inflated unit price** — price far above market/historical rate.
2. **Threshold-skimming** — total just below an approval threshold.
3. **Unfamiliar supplier** — no prior history at the company.
4. **Vendor concentration** — too much recent spend going to one supplier.
5. **Conflict of interest** — hints of a personal relationship between requester and supplier.
6. **Approval irregularity** — wrong approver level, or suspiciously fast approval.
7. **Quote manipulation** — single quote accepted when competitive bids would be normal.
8. **Bank-account change** — supplier recently updated payment details (BEC fraud pattern).

## Per-Order Decisions

For each order, you submit **two things**:

1. **Normal** or **Suspicious**.
2. A **confidence rating** (1 = not at all, 7 = extremely confident).
