# Group-Specific Instructions — Group 3 (AI Evidence)

You have been assigned to **Group 3**.

## What You Will See

For every order, you will see the same three sections as everyone else
(*Order Details*, *Free-text Notes*, *Section E deviations*), **plus**
one additional panel labelled **AI Noteworthy Features**:

> **AI Noteworthy Features (4 items)**
>
> | Feature | Current value | Reference | Why noteworthy |
> | ------- | ------------- | --------- | -------------- |
> | …       | …             | …         | …              |

The AI (a small open-weights language model, Qwen3-8B) has been asked
to read the order plus a few similar historical orders, and to **list
the 4 most noteworthy features** of the current order.

A *noteworthy feature* may be one of two things:

1. **A deviation** — the value is different from typical, e.g.
   *"Unit price $0.29 vs market median $0.12, about 2.5× higher."*
2. **A confirming observation** — the value is consistent with typical,
   e.g. *"Quantity 10 matches the historical median for this SKU."*

The AI will **not** give you an overall verdict, recommendation, or
conclusion. It will not say "suspicious" or "normal". It is providing
you with raw observations only.

## How to Approach the Task

Read the 4 features alongside Section E and the free-text notes. Decide
yourself whether the picture as a whole points to *Normal* or
*Suspicious*. Some orders will have all 4 features looking deviated and
still be normal (genuine business explanations). Some will have 3 out
of 4 looking confirming and still be suspicious (the one deviation
matters most). The synthesis is your job.

## Practice Round

You will start with **2 practice questions** in this same format. After
each practice question you will see whether your final decision was
correct (*Normal* / *Suspicious*).

The practice round is also useful for getting comfortable with reading
4 features at once.

## After the 32 Official Questions

You will be asked a short **5-question Likert survey** about your
experience using the AI's noteworthy-features panel. There are no right
or wrong answers; we want your honest impression.

## Reminder

- 32 official questions, no feedback during the official block.
- One submission per order. **No going back.**
- Aim for 45 to 75 seconds per question.

Press *Continue* to start the practice round.
